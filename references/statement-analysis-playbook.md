# Statement Analysis Playbook

Start with the statements before commentary.

## Income Statement

Ask:

- is growth volume-driven, price-driven, mix-driven, or acquisition-driven?
- are margins rising because operations improved or because expenses shifted elsewhere?
- is profit growth stronger than revenue growth for a clean reason?
- are non-operating items carrying earnings?

Watch items:

- other income
- fair-value gains
- government subsidies
- asset disposal gains
- unusually low tax rate
- minority-interest swings

## Balance Sheet

Ask:

- is growth consuming working capital?
- are receivables and inventory growing faster than sales?
- is goodwill rising faster than earned returns justify?
- is leverage increasing despite healthy profit?

Watch items:

- receivables days
- inventory days
- payables days
- contract liabilities
- short-term debt versus cash
- tangible equity versus reported equity

## Cash Flow

Ask:

- does operating cash flow support earnings?
- is capex maintenance or expansionary?
- is the company borrowing to pay dividends or buy back stock?
- how dependent is the business on external financing?

Useful checks:

- CFO / net income
- FCF / net income
- capex / depreciation
- interest paid / operating profit
- debt maturities within 12 months

## Multi-Period Pattern Recognition

Across 5 years and 8 quarters, look for:

- step changes after acquisitions or accounting changes
- repeated Q4 clean-ups or reserve releases
- deteriorating conversion despite strong earnings
- margin expansion driven by lower reinvestment rather than better economics
- growth supported by extended credit terms
