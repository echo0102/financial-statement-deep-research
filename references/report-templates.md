# Report Templates

Use these as starting points. Trim sections that do not add value.

## Light Report

1. Latest period
   - report date
   - revenue, profit, cash flow, and balance-sheet headline
   - one sentence on what improved or deteriorated
2. Five-year annual view
   - revenue and profit trend
   - margin trend
   - cash conversion and leverage trend
3. Eight-quarter view
   - seasonality and volatility
   - whether the latest quarter is a true inflection or a noisy period
4. Accounting-quality view
   - accruals versus cash
   - working-capital pressure
   - debt and refinancing
   - important note items
5. Conclusion
   - strongest evidence
   - biggest concern
   - what to verify next

## Deep Report

1. Executive conclusion
2. Statement-by-statement analysis
3. Note-level parameters and hidden risks
4. Five-year and eight-quarter bridge
5. Peer comparison
6. Industry cycle
7. Valuation
8. Shareholders, buybacks, dividends, incentives
9. Risks, variants, and monitoring list

## Buffett-Style Variant

1. What business economics do the statements imply
2. Can returns be understood without heroic assumptions
3. Does cash confirm earnings
4. How much leverage or accounting discretion is supporting the result
5. Is management allocating capital rationally
6. What part of the result is cyclical versus durable

## Risk Memo Variant

1. Primary risk statement
2. Statement evidence
3. Note evidence
4. Cycle and financing exposure
5. Triggers that invalidate the risk case
